Love You For A Long Time

Maggie Rogers

“So fucking dainty,” is how Maggie Rogers has described her rise to fame. By now the story seems trite: Pharrell cried after hearing a demo of her song “Alaska” in a masterclass, the video of which went viral, leading to her signing a multi-record deal with Capitol Records. Her debut album Heard it in a Past Life chronicles the tumult of emotions that she experienced in going from English student to rock star overnight.

Today, Rogers is out with “Love You For A Long Time,” her first single since Past Life, in what feels like the start of a new chapter. The track itself is a return to form for Rogers. Where Past Life at times felt over-produced and pigeon holed into Billboard friendly sounds, “Love You For A Long Time” returns to the airy, natural inspired sounds of “Alaska.” Gentle coos over a simple high hat are met with a twangy guitar riff as Maggie’s full bodied voice charges ahead, overcome with emotion. (“I feel it in my body, know it my mind / Oh, I / I’m gonna love you for a long time.”) If “Alaska” was a water color of blues and violets, “Love You For A Long Time,” finds Rogers painting in streaks of orange and yellow. It’s all quite infectious and moving, if a little dull.

Ostensibly a love letter, the song is vague as to its recipient. There are hints that it’s a significant other (“I keep getting lost deep inside your diamond eyes”) but they play as metaphorical more than literal. There’s a transience to her love, an ephemerality in the simple acoustic guitar and the lyrics. It’s not love forever, it’s for a long time (or “a long long long long time,” as she rambles in the final chorus). It sounds less like Rogers has found a special someone than it sounds like she’s finally put the daintiness of the Pharrell saga behind her, that Past Life is finally in the past. Maybe not forever, but at least for now, Rogers is in love with her fans, with her music, and at long last, with her life.
